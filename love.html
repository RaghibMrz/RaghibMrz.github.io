Love?

Or is it just chemical reactions...

It makes you do foolish things. And even if you have an IQ of 160+, you can shoot yourself in the chest by inducing this state of endorphin hyper-secretion upon yourself. The wiseman effect- in lay terms, states that if you tell yourself you love someone, your brain actually starts to believe it. Which begs the question: is it ever a natural thing for us to `fall in love` with someone, or do we always succumb to some form of self-pressure which we place on ourselves; I must conform to the expectation that I bear an on-going, romantic relationship with this human being whose functional benefit to me was initially to just aid me in my goal of gene preservation. The human is not a particularly rational being, so perhaps without this innate inclination towards another human being, would not be smart enough to move towards sexual reproduction and would risk extinction.

Many `geniuses` on the big screen love the narrative that love does not exist, and that the feeling which many humans recall is just a displaced chemical reaction (endorphins, oxytocin... the lot of them), which in earlier stages of our evolutationary stage informed primates how to procreate effectively.
